# Photos — where they go and how to prepare them

Drop image files into the folder for their slot, then tell whoever's editing the
site the filename + a one-line caption/description so it can be wired in.

## Folders and target shapes

| Folder | Used for | Shape | Suggested size | Example filename |
|--------|----------|-------|----------------|------------------|
| `people/` | Member & staff portraits | Square (1:1) | 600 × 600 | `mohammad-eissa.jpg` |
| `people/` | Group photo (People page banner) | Wide (21:9) | 1600 × 686, must be named `group.jpg` | `group.jpg` |
| `news/` | Photo on a News update card | Landscape (16:9) | 1200 × 675 | `2026-05-summer-social.jpg` |
| `gallery/` | "Life in the group" gallery tiles | Landscape (4:3) | 1200 × 900 | `2026-05-lab-visit.jpg` |

The site crops to these shapes automatically, but framing the subject near the
centre gives the best result.

## Preparation
- **Format:** JPG for photos (PNG only for graphics/logos).
- **File size:** aim for under ~400 KB each — compress before adding. Large
  originals make the site slow to load.
- **Colour:** sRGB. **Orientation:** bake in rotation (no sideways photos).

## Naming
- Lower-case, words separated by hyphens, no spaces or accents.
- Portraits: `firstname-lastname.jpg`.
- Events: start with the date, `YYYY-MM-description`, e.g. `2026-06-conference-poster.jpg`.

## For every photo, provide
1. **Caption** — a short line shown under gallery photos / on news cards.
2. **Alt text** — a plain description of what's in the photo, for screen readers
   (required for accessibility). E.g. "Four group members at a poster session."

## ⚠️ Consent before publishing
This is a public website. Before adding any photo that shows identifiable
people (especially students and event attendees), confirm they're happy for it
to appear online. Keep a note of who has agreed.
