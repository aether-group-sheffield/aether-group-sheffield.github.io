Healthcare challenges, engineering solutions

AETHER (Advanced, Effective, Translational Health Engineering Research) is a research group at the School of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, University of Sheffield. We bring together a broad range of engineering expertise to address challenges in healthcare across artificial intelligence, medical devices, automatic control, and hardware.

Our research themes

Artificial Intelligence

Applying machine learning and data-driven methods to healthcare problems, from analysing clinical and physiological data to supporting decision-making, focusing on safety, transparency, and trust.

Learn more →

Medical Devices

Designing and developing instrumentation and devices for monitoring, diagnosis, and treatment, taking ideas from concept through to clinically useful technology.

Learn more →

Automatic Control

Using control engineering and systems modelling to understand physiology and to build closed-loop technologies that respond to patients in real time.

Learn more →

Hardware

Engineering embedded, FPGA, and ASIC implementations that make health technologies secure and efficient, from low-power devices to protecting sensitive health data.

Learn more →
