Healthcare challenges, engineering solutions

AETHER — Advanced, Effective, Translational Health Engineering Research — is a research group at the School of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, University of Sheffield. We bring together a broad range of engineering expertise to address challenges in healthcare, spanning artificial intelligence, medical devices, and automatic control.

What we do

Our research themes

The group's expertise covers three connected areas. Each one is driven by real clinical needs and developed in collaboration with healthcare partners.

Artificial Intelligence

Applying machine learning and data-driven methods to healthcare problems — from analysing clinical and physiological data to supporting decision-making — with a focus on safety, transparency, and trust.

Learn more →

Medical Devices

Designing and developing instrumentation and devices for monitoring, diagnosis, and treatment — taking ideas from concept through to clinically useful technology.

Learn more →

Automatic Control

Using control engineering and systems modelling to understand physiology and to build closed-loop technologies that respond to patients in real time.

Learn more →
How we work

From clinical need to working technology

Translation is in our name. The group's work is organised around moving ideas out of the lab and towards practical use in healthcare.

Start with the challenge

We work with clinicians, patients, and healthcare organisations to understand real problems before proposing solutions.

Engineer the solution

We draw on the group's range of expertise — AI, devices, and control — to design, build, and rigorously evaluate candidate technologies.

Translate into practice

We collaborate with academic, clinical, and industry partners to carry research outputs towards real-world use and impact.
